design ideas


a simple scratch diagram

The rather simple scratch diagram shows a "memory interface" to the fpga. Each triacdriver has got it's own address and the triac trigger delay can be entered by a ten bit positive integer in the range of a 0 to 1024. A half cycle of AC (10 mSecs in our 220 V public current) is divided by 1024 and the integer describes the point of time in this interval.

The zeropass detector is needed because the triacdriver needs to synchronise with the AC timing.

The fpga finally transmits the trigger current pulse to the triac driver with a trigger duration of approx 5 uSecs.
