design ideas


a simple scratch diagram

The rather simple scratch diagram shows a "memory interface" to the fpga. Each triacdriver has got it's own address and the triac trigger delay can be entered as a ten bit positive integer in the range of  0 to 1024. A half cycle of AC (10 mSecs in our 220 V public current) is divided by 1024 and the integer describes the point of time in this interval when triac trigger takes place.

The zeropass detector is needed because the triacdriver needs to be synchronised with the AC timing.

The fpga finally transmits the different trigger current pulses to the triac drivers with a trigger duration of approx 5 uSecs. To be able to switch highly inductive loads, a pulse train will be sent after the initial pulse with an interval time of approx. 100 uSecs, what means approx 10 pulses per mSec.
